Switch 97 October Issue - Emotional Voice

Faye's Style Makes Her Unique

Faye Wong, who was born in Beijing and is 28 years old, has become popular as well in Japan by a Wong Kar-wai directed movie "Chungking Express". A decade ago, she first came to Hong Kong, which she regarded just as a passage to go studying abroad. For some accidental reasons, she debuted as a singer there to be the top Asian artist who is now respected by Europe and American artists as well.

Since there has not been any independent "artists" in the Europe-American sense in Hong Kong show business, Faye's very way of life has caused a lot of sensation and controversies. The more faithful to her belief she acts, the more frictions occur, and strange rumors about her intention spread. To act faithfully to one's own principle -- such a simple thing couldn't be tolerated in the world of the show business. Considering the level of gossip-chasing mass media in Hong Kong and Taiwan, her negative attitude toward any promotions and interviews is reasonable. Even her simplest hope for working contentedly and cherishing her style of music has given rise to strange rumors, only to have established her image in people's brains that she is irritable and selfish.

During our interview and photographing session in Beijing, however, she arrived at studio in advance to make up; though I have met her several times,she has never been late or moody, other staffs said so, too. She just hates to be forced to do what she doesn't accept.

Settled in Beijing

I heard the news that she had left Hong Kong and settled at a new apartment in Beijing, just when people began to wondering if she would come back to the world of music as she had stopped all the work ever since the release of "Anxiety (Fuzao)" in the summer of 1996.

In Beijing, she restored her private life with her partner Dou Wei and other close friends. This change of her surrounding seems to make her relaxed; staffs close to Faye says that she now never insists her opinion when others go on talking. It is said that in previous record company, Faye was forced to waste a lot of energy just in speaking out her own idea, only to see it denied. Faye says that whether her demand can be fulfilled was one of the focus in the process of negotiation with EMI.

"I have been too ignorant so far. This experience made me notice that it is I myself who have to fight for freedom of myself."

The Interview Under Relaxed Atomosphere

For the past year, she has been awfully busy in all the stuff ranging from childbirth to establishment of her own production company; now that she has completed the recording for the latest album, I could have an opportunity to meet her in Beijing calmly. Although she is famous for her short answer in interviews, she talked about her feeling very honestly this time.

When the interview began, she smiled finding the names of the people who helped Faye in her early days on her profile document made by me as a part of data for the interview. The atomosphere was quite a lot relaxed.

The photographing session preceding the interview was conducted at "Jazz-ya" in Sanlitun area which is riched in fashionable shops. Though she was at first a little perplexed at the direction of the photographer Fuzishiro Meisa (in Hong Kong, artists rarely communicate a camera man but just pose as they like), she after all seemed to have enjoyed the session. She also agreed to take a picture on the street's intersection and accepted the ask by passer-bys for her autograph. I guess that such relaxed Faye can't be seen in Hong Kong. This should be the first time for her to take photos in her hometown since the album "Coming Home".

New Record Company and New Album

--How long has it been since you left Hong Kong and moved into Beijing?

"I left Hong Kong last year. But I don't have such a feeling as 'moved' because I have been living in Beijing when there's no works."

--What kind of changes you recognize ever since you transferred to EMI?

"I think things can change wherever you are and whenever it is, and things are always keep changing in the natural flow. There are nothing in particular that I find changed, though people might feel some changes. I don't try to change, either, just because I transferred to EMI."

--You produce all of the songs in "Anxiety(Fuzao)" in Beijing. How about the latest one?

"Only three songs were produced in Beijing. Others were in Hong Kong."

 --Was it because you like to choose where to produce according to each songs?

"What I recorded in Beijing was recorded in collaboration with musicians in Beijing. Since they had trouble going to Hong Kong, I decided to do in Beijing."

--Which songs were recorded in Beijing?

"The first three songs."

--Where did you record your vocal parts?

"Produced in Beijing, recorded in Beijing, and same for Hong Kong. The recording in Hong Kong was directed by Alvin Leung."

--He also took part in the establishment of your production company.

"He and I have gotten along for a long time since my debut. He knows both what I want and what fits the market very well. He knows better about the market than I."

　

--Did Dou Wei take part in the produce this time?

"He played drums for the songs recorded in Beijing."

--I personally like his music as well as your collaboration.

"I don't intend to collaborate with him forcedly. Whenever we have an inspiration, we'll do. He helped me a lot this time again, mainly by taking care of my daughter."

--You didn't write lyrics or compose music at all this time. Why?

"I don't know... I think I'll make several songs next time."

How to Write Lyrics and How to Compose

--You have said that lyrics aren't important very much in interviews before, haven't you?

"Right. For me, music is an image. Lyrics are not important. If music itself convey messages, lyrics are unnecessary, though I don't try to get rid of lyrics... If right words don't occur to me, there's no need to put lyrics compulsorily."

--Please tell me how you compose songs. When any melodies occur to you, do you put it on the score or record it on a tape? Or play it by an instruments?

"All the three are incorrect. In fact, I don't do anything. If it is really good, it should be memorized. If I forget that melody, then it's not a good one."

--Sounds reasonable. But when you produce it, you have to convey it to others.

"I sing for them."

--And staffs memorize it. Do they put it on score?

"It's up to them!"

--Do you use score at a recording studio?

"I can read score, but I don't like to use."

Faye's Way of Singing

--Let me ask about your style of singing. Is it just a natural way for you, or you created it through trial and error?

"My style of singing is neither an inherent one nor my habit. It is originated in uncountable singers whose songs I have heard since I was born, and I think the style is suitable for my kind of music. I have learned from a lot of artists -- some influenced me very much and others just a little. I think singers can treat their voice as instruments. How nice if you master this "instrument" and create music you really like! There's a long way to go for me, though..."

--Your mother is also a singer, isn't she?

"Yes. But I've never been influenced by her."

--Have you listened to songs of Teresa Teng since your childhood?

"Yes. She had influenced me very much. But I don't want to talk about Telesa or Elizabeth from Cocteau Twins in this kind of opportunity. I've learned from a lot of artists, but none in particular, though listeners might feel special influence of particular artists."

"Yes. I don't know which is the main melody, either."

Lyrics and Language

--All the songs in your latest album are sung in Mandarin. Are you planning to sing only in Mandarin from now on, or Mandarin and Cantonese in turn just as before?

"I'd like to sing in Mandarin."

--I think Mandarin has very beautiful sounds.

"Me, too. I hope that all the fans feel so, too. Since Mandarin is my mother tongue, I have a better command of it than Cantonese. Cantonese is a dialect, and have a lot of variations, so there's a limit to sing in Cantonese."

--Do you have any plan to sing in English?

"I might sing several songs in English, but I don't want to make an all-English album."

--How about Japanese?

--You also sing just in scat or senseless words. Are there any difference to them and foreign languages?

"What I sing is what occurred to me, so they are completely different from foreign languages. "

--I see. By the way, many people in Japan or England etc. listen to your songs though they don't understand Mandarin. I was surprised to see many audiences who weren't Chinese when I went to your concert held in Malaysia. I think your music can convey messages without the help of language.

"I hope so."

"I don't speak Japanese at all! You can't sing with simple greetings such as "Hazimemashite (Nice to meet you)" or "Doumo arigato(Thank you very much)"! (laughter) When I sing in languages which I don't understand, I can't put an emotion into it. It's senseless for me."

Recording in Beijing

--Now, would you tell me about how you record in a studio? When in Beijing, you must do recording work with your familiar friends. Do you sing along with their accompaniment?

"Yes. There are two ways of recording: one is I tell them the melody line and they arrange it, the other is I'm involved in an arrangement as well as a composition."

--When I listened to "Anxiety", which was recorded all in Beijing, I could sense the intimacy between you and all the musician. As for what was recorded in Hong Kong, on the other hand, it sounds like musicians play the accompaniment part without listening to your vocals. That's why I asked you the above question.

"You're right. In Beijing, I'm always in a studio during recording, working together with staffs. In Hong Kong, I don't come to a studio until they finish the preparation work."

--I also like the coolness of the songs produced in Hong Kong, but, in the future, which do you think will be the main base for production?

"As a matter of course, I'd prefer to work in Beijing with my close friends. But since I have to release albums at a certain pace, and I'm not good at doing something steadily, I should produce also in Hong Kong."

　

--Do you compose the music at home or at a studio?

"At home. I usually decide all the basics at home, though sometimes I arrange background vocals as I record at a studio."

　

From Now On...

--How often you are going to release albums from now on?

"I'm going to release once in about ten months."

--So you get able to produce rather slowly?

"Yes."

--I've heard that you founded the production office for yourself. Is it because you want to get involved in producing wholly?

"Right."

--What is your role in the office?

"Though I'm supposed to be a "chairman", I don't get involved in daily deskwork. I founded my own production office in order to control the direction of production as well. But there's still a long way to go... I have not made the most of its potential yet."

--You once told that you want to produce an album by yourself in the future. Are you still thinking so?

"I hope I can... But since I have to fulfill the contract with EMI, it's almost impossible for me to compose enough songs to fill one album in time. Anyway I dedicate myself to improve the work little by little. I don't think my latest album is a perfect one."

--Are there any plans of concerts in the near future?

"None this year. But maybe I'll give some concerts next year."

--Will it be held in several places as concert tour? Or just in Hong Kong for a rather long period?

"As I don't like to perform in one place too long, it'll be a concert tour, if I do."

--I've heard that you don't get involved in jacket design nowadays. Is that true?

"Yes. Recently, I didn't have a good inspiration for design, nor an interest. If I can make an extremely good album in the future, some ideas might occur to me."

In Search of Her Original Style

Produced in last summer, "Anxiety" (Fuzao) is virtually her first self-producing album, which is recorded in Beijing with many of her close friends, different from any of her earlier produced-in-Hong Kong albums. The concept of this epoch-making album is quite simple, and we can hear her comfortable voice with its alternative melody lines. Beautifully rhyming lyrics are full of significance in spite of their simplicity. Some songs don't have any lyrics, which make us feel as if we were floating in the air. Although she admits that Hong Kong and Taiwan are much more advanced in terms of technology such as recording or mixing, she is eager to produce in Beijing with her familiar partners. However it isn't that easy; her latest album "Faye Wong" contains more songs recorded in Hong Kong than in Beijing. In comparison with "Anxiety", her brand-new album might seem a little bit retrograde. But her voice really gets softer and more unique than ever. As Faye said in the interview, she is sure to progress continuously. Now that I'm sure that Faye won't quit singing, I will follow her forever to see how she gets to her destination.

Her Life in Beijing

Superstar as she is in Hong Kong, she enjoys just a normal life in Beijing. She lives at an apartment in Chaoyang district, which is very frugal housing in comparison with high-class residential district in other countries. "Faye hopes to live in normal house and lead a normal life. To put too much burden on her husband and daughter is the last thing she wants to do. She has been caring a lot to realize that normal life", said many of her close friends. That may be the reason she rarely performs in Beijing.

-Do you make songs for your daughter?

"Not so often; perhaps I'm not a respectable mother!"

--Don't you sing for her at the bedside?

"Yeah, I play my CDs and sometimes sing for her."

--Are there any place you'd like to live?

"I don't think I'll move out of Beijing. But I like traveling around."

--Does where to live influence your music?

"Since Beijing is my hometown, it doesn't excite me very much, while Hong Kong and other strange cities inspire me a lot."

--You've been to Japan, haven't you?

"Yes. I've been to Tokyo twice and Fukuoka (a major city in southern Japan). To tell you the truth, I'm not so fond of Tokyo. Tokyo looks like Hong Kong for me -- it is crowded and makes me tensed. I'd prefer Fukuoka."

--You once said that you wanted to go to Egypt.

"I've been longing for Egypt since I was a child, but I haven't been there yet."
