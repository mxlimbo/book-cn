hank you to the others who have added to this. Their questions and tips Have been a lot of help, so I will just add where I think it may be of some help.

Take your time. There is plenty of time to do the test. If this is your first One, go ahead and take the practice test. It does not take away from your Time, and it is a nice, relaxing way to start. And, it gives you a successful Start, (it gives you all the answers, so you get to complete a test with !00%

Correct before you start the real one.) And you might learn a couple things that you ignored in 8th grade :) Think only about how successful you will feel _when_ you pass the test. If, for some reason, you don't pass it this time, just look at it as a learning Experience. Sit down immediately, in the room outside or on the curb, and Write down the things you need to study up on. I talked with one person That was really devastated, and when he began to realize he could go home, With the information fresh, and study up on what more he might need, he left With a new resolve. It is just a test, after all. Not like anything that will Really change your life

If you use the NewRiders book for Windows 95, be sure and go to their site And get the errata - and realiz it should probably be, say, a page or two longer. Overall, however, spending a lot of time with the book, and verifying info with the 95 Resource Kit kit and win95rk.hlp (on the CD) will get you through What would you answer to questions referencing "PCL" printers? Probably Want to find a source outside of this book to make it clear. Someone might Ask you 3 questions referencing PCL printers someday.

Don't let a question about user-level access on a 95 network throw you, If no NT or Novell server is mentioned. But be very cautious about doing that anywhere else. And spend a lot of time with the options inside Policy Editor. Try changing all the Options on your home network, (you do have one, don't you??!!) and changing them

Back, maybe developing some mnemonic to help remember. You may get Exhibits that refresh your memory, but you may not.
