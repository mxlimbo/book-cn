Overview of Dial-Up Networking

You can use Dial-Up Networking in many ways (see Figure 18.1). For example, in the following situations:

  • Your company employs salespeople, support personnel, or other employees who often travel to customer sites and require remote access to the company network. These remote users can use Dial-Up Networking to connect to the network from their Windows 98-based notebook computers.

  • Your company is not physically connected to the Internet, but some users require occasional Internet access. You can use Dial-Up Networking to connect to the Internet on demand.

  • Your company wants to provide remote access to a user's onsite computer. You can use Dial-Up Networking to make the user's onsite computer a Dial-Up Server, so the user can connect to it remotely.
