Practice: Managing Print Tasks in Windows 98

In this practice, you will add documents to your local print queue, pause the print job, and cancel it.

Important

To complete this exercise, you must have installed a local printer.

    To add documents to the print queue

    1. Click Start, point to Find, and then click Files or Folders.

      The Find dialog box appears.

    2. In the Named box, type *.bmp, and then click Find Now.

      Windows 98 searches your hard drive for all files with the extension .bmp.

    3. Click Start, point to Settings, and then click Printers.

    4. In the Printers windows, double-click on your local printer.

      Your local printer windows opens.

    5. Select the first three files in the list of found files and drag them to the top of the your local printer window.

      A dialog box appears, asking if you are sure you want to print multiple files at once.

    6. Click Yes.

      The documents appear as print jobs in the local printer window.

    7. Close the Find dialog box.

    To pause and cancel a print job

    1. In the local printer window, right-click one of the queued jobs, and then click Pause Printing.

      The status of the print job should now be paused.

    2. In the local printer window, right-click one of the queued jobs, and then click Cancel Printing.

      The status of the print job should now be cancelled. The other two print jobs remain queued and will now be scheduled and run automatically.
